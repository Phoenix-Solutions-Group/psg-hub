# ADR-004: Make the survey-alert suppression rule explicit

## Status

Proposed

## Context

A core rule of the FileMaker feedback loop is suppression: when a customer has an
open survey alert, they are removed from routine mail so PSG does not send a
cheerful thank-you or warranty letter to an unhappy customer. In FileMaker this
was an emergent side effect of an `IsEmpty(survey alert)` guard inside the
eligibility finds. The DDR proved the behavior exists but showed it was invisible:
nothing in the data recorded that a customer was suppressed, or why. That makes it
impossible to audit, easy to break accidentally during a rebuild, and impossible
to report on.

## Decision

Make suppression explicit and queryable. The `letter_eligibility` table carries a
`suppressed_by_alert` boolean alongside `eligible`. When eligibility is computed,
a customer with an open survey alert in the window is marked `eligible = false`
and `suppressed_by_alert = true`, recording both the outcome and the reason in the
row. Suppression becomes a stored, auditable fact instead of an emergent join
behavior.

## Consequences

**Positive**

- Suppression is auditable: a reviewer can see exactly who was held back from mail
  and why.
- It is reportable: the survey-alert and eligibility reports can count suppressed
  customers directly.
- It is harder to break silently during the rebuild, because the rule is a named
  column with an acceptance criterion, not a hidden side effect.

**Negative / trade-offs**

- One extra column and one extra compute step in the eligibility cron.
- The suppression window definition (which alert classes, over what interval) must
  be specified precisely, because it is now visible and will be checked.

## Alternatives considered

- **Keep suppression as an emergent join filter.** Rejected: it reproduces the
  invisible behavior the DDR flagged and leaves no audit trail.
- **Record suppression only in cron logs, not in the row.** Rejected: logs are not
  queryable alongside the eligibility data and do not survive as the durable
  record an audit or report needs.

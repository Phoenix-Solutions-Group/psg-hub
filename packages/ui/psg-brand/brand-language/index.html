01 Philosophy

Understated luxury.
Precision craft.

PSG operates at the intersection of strategic insight and operational execution. Our brand reflects that duality — authoritative yet approachable, refined yet practical. Every design decision should feel deliberate, measured, and earned.

01

Trust

Earned through consistency and follow-through. We don't sell promises — we deliver measurable outcomes, then let the work speak.

02

Innovation

Driven by practical solutions, not hype. We adopt what works, refine what doesn't, and never chase trends for their own sake.

03

Heritage

35 years of transformation expertise. We've earned the right to be confident — not because we say so, but because the track record is there.

04

Refinement

Attention to detail in every touchpoint. From the tracking on an eyebrow to the timing of a hover state — precision is the signature.

The method

Clean structured layouts. Ample whitespace. Precise alignment. Subtle depth. Functional elegance. These are not aesthetic preferences — they are operational principles. Every element earns its place.

The audience

Owner-operators and marketing leaders at small-to-medium businesses, with a specialty in collision repair shops, MSO operators, and the adjacent automotive aftermarket. People who measure success in cycle time, CSI scores, and lifetime value — not impressions.

02 Logo & Identity

The phoenix mark.

The PSG identity combines a distinctive phoenix flame with bold geometric letterforms. The mark represents transformation — our founding promise and enduring capability. Eleven variants ship in three families: horizontal lockups for navigation and headers, vertical lockups for posters and decks, and the mark alone for icons and small-format use.

Clear space

Maintain a minimum clear zone equal to the height of the "P" letterform around all edges. No elements should encroach on this boundary.

Minimum size

The full lockup should never appear smaller than 120px wide on screen or 30mm in print. The flame mark alone: 24px / 8mm minimum.

Color restrictions

Use only the primary (midnight + ember) or reverse (paper + ember) colorways. Never recolor the flame. Never place the logo on a busy or low-contrast background.

04 Typography

Gotham leads. Didact Gothic reads.

Two typefaces do all the work. Gotham carries the authority — headlines, eyebrows, buttons, and navigation. Didact Gothic carries the warmth — body copy, form fields, and long-form reading. The heaviest Gotham weight used for headings is Bold (700). Licensed via Adobe Fonts.

Body copy — Didact Gothic

Strategic insight, operationally executed. We've built a practice around collision repair marketing because the category demands precision — from the way an estimator answers the phone to the words on a Google review response.

Supporting text is set at 14px in Mist for secondary information, captions, and helper text. Line height remains generous at 1.55.

Weight ceiling

Headings top out at 700 (Gotham Bold) — the brand’s 2026 refresh swapped the old thin display style for confident, weighty headlines. H4 and below drop to Medium (500) to keep hierarchy clear.

Negative tracking

At display sizes (48px+), apply -0.02em tracking. This tightens the geometric letter-spacing of Gotham at scale, giving headlines a composed, editorial feel.

Line height

Display: 1.0–1.05. Body: 1.65. Lead body: 1.6. Generous line height is non-negotiable — it's the single biggest factor in making long text feel approachable.

05 Voice & Tone

A senior consultant in a tailored sport coat.

Warm, precise, and quietly confident. Editorial, never salesy. PSG copy breathes — short sentences interleaved with one longer. Every adjective earns its place. No exclamation points outside of error states.

PSG does

  • Second person for value props — "your shop," "your team"
  • First-person plural for capabilities — "we," "our"
  • Sentence case for body, Title Case for page headers
  • Em-dashes for asides — like this
  • Oxford comma, always
  • Spell out one through nine, numerals for 10+
  • Contractions sparingly — "we'll," "you're"

PSG does not

  • Use exclamation points outside error states
  • Use emoji — anywhere, ever
  • Write ALL-CAPS body copy
  • Use "I" — PSG is a team
  • Use contractions in headlines or pull quotes
  • Write paragraphs longer than four lines
  • Use five-clause headlines or wall-of-text

06 Photography

Documentary warmth. Natural light.

Imagery enters as deliberate full-bleed moments, not pattern fill. When photographs appear, they should feel like they were taken by a skilled documentary photographer — not pulled from a stock library.

Hero imagery placeholder Warm, documentary, shallow DoF
Detail / craft shot Slightly desaturated, natural light

Color grade

Warm shadows, controlled highlights. Slightly desaturated overall. Never cool or blue-shifted. The warmth should match the paper tone of the brand.

Subject matter

Craftspeople at work. Real shops, real cars, real bays. Shallow depth of field preferred. Hands, tools, and process shots convey the operational nature of the work.

Protection gradient

When text overlays photography, apply a midnight protection gradient — 0% alpha at top to 60% alpha at bottom. This ensures legibility without obscuring the image.

07 Layout & Grid

Twelve columns. Generous whitespace.

The layout system is built on a 12-column grid with 24px gutters. Asymmetric editorial splits (5/7 or 4/8) are preferred over symmetric halves for marquee sections. Whitespace is the signature — never compress it.

Asymmetric splits

Marquee sections use 5/7 or 4/8 column splits. The wider column carries the main content; the narrower column holds supporting material, stats, or imagery.

Section rhythm

96–128px vertical padding on desktop sections, 48–64px on mobile. Hairline dividers between sections at 1px stone. Dark sections break the rhythm — use them for proof or CTAs.

10 Motion & Animation

Refined ease-out. No bounce.

Animation is purposeful and restrained. Every transition uses the same easing curve. The brand does not wiggle, bounce, or overshoot. Hover the circles below to see the three duration tiers.

The easing curve

cubic-bezier(0.22, 0.61, 0.36, 1) — a refined ease-out. Quick departure, gentle settle. Used for all hover, press, and reveal transitions.

Scroll-in pattern

Fade in + 8px translate-up. That's it. Applied on first appearance only — no repeat on re-scroll. No parallax, no scale effects, no confetti.

11 Iconography

Lucide. 2px stroke. 24px grid.

Lucide icons are chosen for their consistent stroke weight, rounded line caps, and editorial restraint. Icons are never filled — they inherit color from the surrounding text via currentColor.

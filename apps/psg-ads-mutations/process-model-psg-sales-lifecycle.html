4. Customer Foundation - Sales Side

2S3 pre-proposal diagnosticclaude-ads audit + SEMrush competitor scan + review snapshotCurrent Situation section of proposal, anchored in their actual digital footprint
3S5 negotiation pushbackLive conversation about objectionsConcession log + future objection library updates
The Sales team is the first PSG voice the customer hears. Discovery is not a qualification interrogation. It is the first time PSG shows the prospect that we listen better than the agencies they have worked with before. Capture pain in their words, not in PSG categories.

6. Sales Lifecycle Phase Detail

S0 - Prospecting

Owner: SDR + Rex Forecast confidence: 10% Exit: Initial contact made + decision-maker verified
  • Build target list from ICP: independent collision shops, 3-15 bays, geo within PSG service area, no comprehensive marketing system. Source from Apollo, SEMrush overview_research, partner referrals, inbound.
  • Score each account against the qualification scorecard before any outreach. Disqualify: in-house marketing director, current agency on long contract, owner uninterested.
  • Stale-deal rule: no contact attempt within 14 days = stale.

S1 - Outreach

Owner: SDR Forecast confidence: 25% Exit: Discovery meeting scheduled or interest confirmed
  • Multi-touch sequence: 5-7 touches over 14 days across phone, email, LinkedIn. PSG voice (calm, credible, operationally minded).
  • Pre-outreach research: shop's GBP, review count, current paid ads via SEMrush overview_research.
  • Stale-deal rule: no response after 5 attempts in 14 days = Nurture or Closed Lost.

S2 - Discovery

Owner: Rex or AE Forecast confidence: 40% Exit: Pain documented + agreement to proceed
  • 60-90 min discovery call. Shop tour or video walkthrough when possible.
  • Use PSG qualification methodology: Fit, Problem, Urgency, Authority, Economics, Readiness.
  • Capture in Pipedrive: pain points, current marketing tools, growth goals, budget range, decision-maker, DRP relationships, OEM certifications, three named competitors.
  • Special Ad Categories check: confirm not in housing, finance, healthcare.
  • Red flag: budget undiscussable, no decision-maker involvement, growth goals unclear.
  • Stale-deal rule: no follow-up within 10 days = Nurture.

S3 - Solution Design

Owner: Rex + Account Strategist Forecast confidence: 60% Exit: Internal solution approved + proposal presentation scheduled

Sales pulls Delivery in for the diagnostic. The diagnostic becomes the Current Situation section of the proposal.

  • Run claude-ads /ads audit on prospect (if access granted) or in spend-context mode.
  • Run SEMrush organic_research on prospect + 3 top local competitors named in discovery.
  • Pull prospect's Google Business Profile snapshot, review count, rating.
  • Match findings to LocalReach tier (Basic, Plus, Premium). Include add-ons (extra LPs, sGTM, expanded CRO).
  • Get internal approval (Rex + Account Strategist) on tier and any custom scope.

S4 - Proposal

Owner: Rex or AE Forecast confidence: 70% Exit: Prospect acknowledges proposal + provides feedback or schedules review
  • Author proposal using the standard template (see section 7 for full anatomy).
  • Build in PandaDoc: e-signable, trackable (open + section views), SOW and MSA bundled.
  • Deliver live (screen-share walkthrough) rather than emailing a PDF cold. Win rate 2-3x higher.
  • Stale-deal rule: no response in 14 days = follow up twice; if still silent, Nurture or Closed Lost.

S5 - Negotiation

Owner: Rex Forecast confidence: 85% Exit: Terms agreed + signed or verbal commit pending signature
  • Work through objections from PSG objection-library framework.
  • Allowed concessions: shift setup-fee timing, defer one month of monthly retainer for 6-month-instead-of-12 trade.
  • Non-negotiables: ad spend pass-through model, monthly retainer floor, audit log discipline, 14-day stabilization rule.
  • Red flag: over 21 days without progress, unreasonable concessions, insistence on paying Google directly without MCC access.
  • Stale-deal rule: no progress in 21 days = escalate or Closed Lost.

S6 - Contract and Signature

Owner: Rex + Legal (if non-standard) Forecast confidence: 95% Exit: Both parties signed in PandaDoc
  • SOW + MSA bundled in one envelope. Client signs first, PSG counter-signs.
  • Standard MSA clauses to verify: scope, term (12 months default), payment terms (net 30 default), ad spend pass-through, cancellation notice (30 days), data ownership (client owns ad account + LP content), audit log retention (PSG keeps).
  • Collision-specific clauses: insurance carrier exclusion lists, DRP non-interference, geo exclusivity if granted.
  • Non-standard clauses: route through legal:review-contract.

S7 - Commercial Setup

Owner: Finance Lead + Rex Forecast confidence: 100% (signed deal) Exit: Setup fee invoice paid + recurring billing scheduled
  • Create customer in invoiced.app via API: name, billing email, AP contact, payment method (ACH preferred for $3k+ monthly), tax info, signed SOW attached.
  • Issue setup fee invoice via invoiced.app API. Net 0 to net 7 typical. Setup fee must be paid before D1 kickoff.
  • Schedule recurring monthly retainer subscription. First invoice issues on 1st of month following kickoff.
  • Configure dunning workflow: reminders at +3, +7, +14 days; escalation to Account Strategist at +21.
  • Capture autopay authorization (ACH preferred). Manual ACH or check fallback documented.
  • Create Asana project for the client engagement using the asana-project-setup skill. Project name: "[Client Name] - LocalReach [Tier]". Pre-structure with sections for PRE-D1, D1, D2 (with D2a-D2e), D3, D4 (with D4a-D4e), D5, D6 (recurring loops), D7, D8. Add milestones at each phase exit gate. Add Account Strategist as project owner.
  • Bootstrap Obsidian vault for the client using claude-obsidian (/wiki command, Mode C: Business). Vault name: psg-[client-slug]-vault. Configure MCP entry. Seed with the S2 discovery notes, S3 diagnostic findings, named competitors, and three PSG canonical personas selected for this client. Vault stores: personas, VoC, decisions, meetings, learnings (see Section 12 for full structure).
  • Add both the Asana project URL and the Obsidian vault path to the handoff package and the Pipedrive deal record.
  • See section 8 for the full setup detail and section 10 for ongoing operations.

S8 - Closed Won

Owner: Rex Forecast confidence: 100% Exit: Closed Won in Pipedrive + Handoff Gate triggered
  • Mark Closed Won in Pipedrive with: contract signed date, total value (MRR + setup + 12-month), service start date, onboarding owner (Account Strategist).
  • Trigger Handoff Gate (section 9). Do not let Delivery start without it.

7. Proposal Anatomy - What a PSG Proposal Looks Like

Every PSG proposal contains the same eight sections in the same order. Variation lives in scope and pricing tier, not in structure. The standard template lives in psg-sales-team/templates/proposal-document.md.

Required Sections (always present)

#SectionWhat it containsWhy it has to be there
1Cover + Executive SummaryOne paragraph: shop's current state, recommended solution, expected outcome.Decision-makers read this paragraph and skip to pricing. It has to stand alone.
2Current SituationFactual findings from the S3 diagnostic: GBP completeness, review count and rating, current paid ads, local SEO position vs named competitors, site quality. No judgment, no hype.Shows the prospect that PSG looked. Anchors the proposal in their data.
3Recommended SolutionSelected LocalReach tier plus any add-ons. Frames the solution as the answer to the gaps in Section 2.Connects what PSG saw to what PSG will do.
4Scope of WorkTable: deliverable, description, timeline, owner. Both PSG and Client work shown.Removes "what does this include" objections.
5Investment + Commercial TermsSetup fee, monthly retainer, ad spend model, term, payment terms, billing cadence, included vs billed separately.The most-read section after Section 1. Hide nothing.
6Why PSGCollision-only specialization, LocalReach system, audit-log discipline, the team. Three to four sentences max.Credibility without bragging.
7Assumptions, Dependencies, Out-of-ScopeExplicit list: what PSG assumes is true, what PSG depends on (access, SLA), what is NOT included.Prevents 90% of scope disputes in month 3.
8Next Steps + Signature BlockFive numbered steps from sign to launch. SOW + MSA bundled. E-sign block.Removes friction.

What to Explicitly Point Out (don't bury these)

These cause disputes when not surfaced clearly. They go in Section 7 (Assumptions, Dependencies, Out-of-Scope) and are restated verbally in the live walkthrough.
ItemHow to phrase it

Out of scope (always listed by name)"This SOW does not include website redesign, CRM setup, social media management, organic SEO (unless added), email marketing, or in-shop process changes. We are happy to scope any of these separately."

Proposal Quality Checklist (pre-send)

  • Shop name appears correctly in every section header and variable slot.
  • Three competitors named in Section 2 are real and were named by the prospect in S2.
  • KPI targets in Section 3 match the LTV math from the diagnostic, not generic averages.
  • Setup fee + monthly + 12-month total value shown in Section 5.
  • Ad spend pass-through explicitly named in Section 5 AND Section 7.
  • Section 7 lists at least 5 out-of-scope items.
  • SOW + MSA bundled in one PandaDoc envelope.
  • Proposal expiry date set (30 days from send).
  • E-signature block: client first, PSG counter-sign.
  • Voice check: reads like PSG (calm, credible, operationally minded).
  • Final pass against PSG proposal-quality-checklist.md.

Ad Spend Pass-Through Model

StepWhat happensSystem
1PSG card sits on the Google Ads MCC. All ad spend charges PSG monthly.Google Ads billing

The pass-through model gives PSG full control of the ad account (no card-on-file disruption from client) but creates a one-month cash float exposure per client. Finance Lead has to monitor clients whose monthly spend exceeds a credit threshold (typically $5k/mo) and require ACH autopay with shorter terms (net 15) at that scale.

12. Persistent Knowledge in Obsidian (per-client vault)

Every client engagement gets its own Obsidian vault created at S7 alongside the Asana project. Obsidian holds anything that compounds: the persona doc, VoC research, meeting notes, decisions, learnings. Asana holds the operational tasks. Pipedrive holds the CRM state. invoiced.app holds the billing. PandaDoc holds the contracts. Each tool has one job.

The vault is created BEFORE Delivery starts. Account Strategist inherits a vault on day one that already contains the discovery notes, the diagnostic, and the three canonical personas selected for this client.

16. Anti-Patterns to Avoid

Sales anti-patterns

  • Skipping the S3 diagnostic and writing the proposal from intuition. Section 2 reads generic and the prospect notices.
  • Emailing a cold proposal PDF instead of walking through it live. Win rate drops 2-3x.
  • Hiding ad spend pass-through in small print. Causes month-3 disputes when the first ad spend line item appears.
  • Advancing a stage without meeting the exit criteria. Inflates the forecast and rots the pipeline.
  • Negotiating away the audit log discipline or the 14-day stabilization rule. Non-negotiables.
  • Closing without the Handoff Gate. Delivery starts blind and the first month is a mess.

Commercial anti-patterns

  • Letting setup fee go uncollected before kickoff. PSG ends up doing free work and the client takes longer to value the engagement.
  • Not pulling ad spend monthly and reconciling against the Google Ads invoice. Variance compounds.
  • Manual invoicing in invoiced.app UI instead of via API. Inconsistent formatting, no SOW attached, no audit trail.
  • No autopay on high-spend clients. Ad spend float blows up cash position.
  • Pausing campaigns at +30 days without written notice. Becomes a relationship rupture instead of a billing escalation.
  • Skipping the renewal motion until day -10. No time to address pricing, churn risk, or expansion conversation.
  • Cancellation with no exit interview. Lose the learning.
  • Removing PSG card from MCC before confirming client billing. Ads silently stop.

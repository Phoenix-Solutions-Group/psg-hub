Daily Impressions, April vs May

Impressions tracked April closely through May 17, then dropped while Google held ads in review and put the new campaigns through its learning window.
Why the drop after May 17? The drop is on Google's side, not ours. Three things hit at once:
  • Google's ad review queue. Google's automated policy system flagged several of your certified-repair ads in mid-May. While ads sit in that queue, Google throttles or pauses them until a human at Google clears them. We submitted appeals on May 20, and Google has now cleared every ad, but the queue itself was what blocked impressions during those days.
  • Google's new-campaign learning period. Every new campaign goes through a 7 to 14 day learning phase. Google deliberately throttles serving while its algorithm gathers signal. Your 2026 Q2 campaigns started that window mid-May. There is no way to skip it.
  • Smart Campaign reach swings. The May 17 spike and the drop right after came from your Brand Smart campaign, which Google fully manages. Day-to-day reach bounces around while Google's automation tests where to place those ads.

These are normal Google behaviors when an account goes through structural changes and policy reviews at the same time. Appeals are cleared. The learning window is closing. Impressions should normalize through the first half of June.

What We Accomplished This Month

The work, in plain language. Every change went through a dry-run preview before going live, and the before-and-after is logged on our end.

1. Rebuilt the 2026 Q2 Campaign Stack

We finished moving off the old campaigns. Six new ones are live, each one tied to a specific service line: Brand Defense, Local Collision, Tesla Approved, JLR Certified, Toyota Certified, and BMW or Rivian Certified. The legacy campaigns are paused, not deleted, so the history is still there if we need to reference it.

2. Cleaned Up Conversion Tracking

The account was treating Get Directions clicks, Menu views, and Website clicks from your Google Business Profile as conversions. That inflated the reported numbers and gave Google's bidding algorithm the wrong signal about what a real lead looks like. We turned off seven of those micro-events and kept the four that actually mean something: form fills, phone calls from ads, qualified leads, and Smart Campaign tracked calls. Bidding now optimizes for real leads.

3. Restored Brand Defense

The Wallace Collision Center brand campaign was paused when we picked the account up, so nothing was defending the brand on Google search. We turned it back on, switched it to a Maximize Clicks bidding strategy, and added five exact-match brand keyword variations covering Bristol, Kingsport, and the core brand phrases.

4. Resolved All Outstanding Ad Disapprovals

Google had flagged seven ads, three on Toyota creative and four across other certified-repair lines. We worked each one, figured out what the policy concern was, and appealed. Every ad in the account is approved today.

5. Expanded Negative Keyword Coverage

We added 315 negative keywords this month, more than doubling the list. They block dealership and "for sale" queries, parts and DIY repair kits, service and maintenance searches, out-of-area locations, and shopper queries like Tesla news or Land Rover lease deals. More of every click now goes to people actually looking for collision repair.

6. Refreshed Landing Page Mapping

Every 2026 Q2 campaign now points to the right landing page: Tesla to the Tesla page, BMW to the BMW page, JLR to the JLR page, Toyota to the Toyota page. We also fixed the UTM tracking so attribution lines up cleanly.

7. Tightened Up GTM Conversion Tags

There was a redundant pageview tag in your Google Tag Manager that was firing at the same time as the lead conversion tag. We paused it. The remaining tag fires only when a lead actually completes.

8. Expanded Tesla and JLR Keyword Reach

On May 21 we widened the Tesla Approved and JLR Certified campaigns. The original keywords were tight: "tesla certified collision repair", "jaguar certified collision repair", phrases real people don't usually type. We added 13 phrase-match terms in the language people actually use, like "land rover body shop", "tesla collision repair", "range rover body shop". We also raised Tesla's bid guardrail so it can compete on the broader terms.

Opportunities and Suggestions

What is next. All of these work with what is already in the account.

Performance Max pilot

Performance Max needs a baseline of qualified-lead history to work, and the account has that now. It opens up Maps, YouTube, Discover, and Shopping placements. We would run a controlled pilot in early June with brand exclusions set up first, so it does not cannibalize the brand search campaign.

Landing page experience uplift

Google's Quality Score signal says the certified-make landing pages are slow on mobile and a little thin on content. We will put together a punch list for the website team: image compression, structured data markup for LocalBusiness and AutoRepair, mobile load fixes. Better landing pages improve Quality Score, and Quality Score affects ad ranking on every campaign.

Call tracking consolidation

There are four different phone numbers across the call assets right now. We will consolidate to one tracked number so we can actually tell which campaign drove which call.

Lead form extension

Lead Form extensions let someone request a quote without leaving the search results page. For collision repair we typically see 10 to 20 percent more leads. Scoping it for June.

Past-customer remarketing list

A hashed list of past customer emails gives Google a strong audience signal, both to find similar prospects and to let us include or exclude existing customers depending on the campaign. We will ask for a clean export and upload it.

Ad creative refresh on under-rated ads

A few responsive search ads are sitting at "Average" or "Poor" strength in Google's rating. We will rewrite the headlines and descriptions to get every ad to "Good" or "Excellent". Usually worth 10 to 15 percent more clicks.

1. Scope and Boundaries

Starts when: the Handoff Gate completes and D1 kickoff is scheduled. Ends when: the engagement renews, expands, or offboards with a clean handover. Phases D6a through D6d run continuously from launch (D5) until offboard.

5. Collision Persona Library

The PSG library is the starting point. Every collision engagement gets the 8 personas mapped to the client's actual customer mix, then refined with VoC research in D2e.

Primary - 35-45% of traffic
1. General Collision Repair Customer

Stressed, detail-oriented, comparison-shopping. Has time pressure but wants quality. Anxieties in order: trust, quality, cost, time, insurance. Lands on homepage and service pages.

Primary - 20-30% of traffic
2. First-Time Accident Victim

Anxious, overwhelmed, no prior frame of reference. Needs hand-holding. Anxieties: insurance, cost, trust, time, quality. Lands on "what to do after an accident" content.

Primary - 15-25% of traffic
3. Insurance Claimant

Frustrated with insurer, wants an advocate. Knows DRP exists, suspicious of shop steering. Anxieties: insurance, trust, quality, cost, time. Lands on insurance-specific LPs.

Secondary - 5-10% of traffic
4. Cash-Pay Customer

Independent, cost-conscious, deliberately avoiding insurance. Wants a transparent estimate. Anxieties: cost, trust, quality, time. Lands on PDR and minor damage pages.

Secondary - 5-8% of traffic
5. At-Fault Insured Driver

Guilty, worried about premium impact. Needs reassurance plus discretion. Anxieties: insurance (premium), cost, trust, quality, time. Lands on FAQ and insurance guidance pages.

Secondary - 3-5% of traffic
6. Repeat Accident Victim

Frustrated by recurrence, wants efficiency. Knows the process. Anxieties: time, quality, insurance, cost, trust. Lands on "schedule repair" pages with minimal friction.

Tertiary - 2-5% of traffic
7. OEM-Certified Customer

Luxury vehicle owner, demands factory-correct repair. Anxieties: quality (paramount), trust, time, cost. Lands on brand-specific pages (BMW, Mercedes, Audi, Lexus).

Tertiary - 1-3% of traffic, fastest growing
8. OEM-Certified EV Owner

Tech-savvy, eco-conscious, requires EV-trained tech and certified battery handling. Anxieties: quality, trust (EV-specific), time (parts), cost. Lands on Tesla, Rivian, Lucid pages.

7. Delivery Lifecycle Phase Detail

D1 - Onboard and Access

Duration: Days 1-5 post-handoff Owner: Account Strategist + Analytics Engineer Exit gate: All access confirmed, baseline data flowing
  • Take ownership of the Asana project created by Sales at S7. Account Strategist becomes the Project Owner. Review the pre-structured D1-D8 sections, confirm milestone dates, assign team members (Ads Eng, Web Eng, Content Lead, CRO Analyst, Analytics Eng) as project members with appropriate roles.
  • Take ownership of the Obsidian vault created by Sales at S7. Account Strategist becomes the Vault Steward. Verify the MCP entry works (obsidian-vault-[client-slug]), review the seed content (S2 discovery, S3 diagnostic, three selected personas, three named competitors), and run lint the wiki to confirm no orphans. Add Content Lead, CRO Analyst, and Analytics Engineer as vault collaborators if using Obsidian Sync.
  • Send access request bundle: Google Ads MCC link, GA4 admin, GTM publish, Search Console, Sanity editor, Vercel or Netlify deploy access, call tracking admin, CRM read access, review platform admin (GBP, Yelp). Track each access item as an Asana task in the D1 section.
  • Add the customer ID to googleads_psg/.env and to the client registry. Add Sanity project ID and dataset to engagement config.
  • Stand up shared Slack channel + shared Drive folder (Discovery, Strategy, Build, Reports, Audit Logs, VoC). Link the Slack channel and Drive folder to the Asana project description.
  • Schedule recurring weekly check-in (30 min) and monthly review (60 min). Schedule first QBR 90 days out. Add as recurring Asana tasks in D6 / D7 sections.
  • Send client questionnaire from collision-repair-content-system. Get back services, certifications, insurance relationships, top 3 competitors (cross-check against Sales S2 notes), testimonials, geo radius.

D2 - Audit, Discovery, and Baseline

Duration: Days 5-15 Owner: Ads Engineer + Analytics Engineer + Content Lead Exit gate: Combined audit deck delivered and discussed

D2a Ads Account Audit

  • Read-only pull via mcp__google-ads-mcp__search: account structure, 90 days spend, CPA, ROAS, impression share, quality score, search terms, conversion actions.
  • Run /ads audit with local-service template + monthly spend context + collision repair vertical.
  • Run /ads google for the 80-check Google-specific deep dive.
  • Output: written audit + apps/ads/reports/{client}-audit-{date}.pdf.

D2b SEMrush Market and Competitor Intelligence

  • keyword_research on seed terms (collision repair, auto body, ADAS calibration, etc.).
  • organic_research on top 5 local competitors. Pull top 100 organic keywords each.
  • overview_research for client + each competitor (DA, organic traffic value, paid estimate).
  • backlink_research on competitors for link-building opportunities.
  • siteaudit_research on client site for tech SEO baseline (feeds D4b dev backlog).
  • trends_research for seasonal collision demand (hail, deer, winter).
  • Output: keyword gap matrix, local long-tail opportunity list, competitor positioning brief.

D2c Analytics and Tracking Audit

  • GA4 audit: events, conversions, audiences, attribution, BigQuery export, consent mode v2.
  • GTM container audit: triggers, tag firing order, no duplicates, no rogue scripts. sGTM if present.
  • Call tracking audit: DNI working, recordings retained, GA4 events firing, offline import working.
  • Search Console verified, sitemap submitted, coverage report scanned.
  • Conversion Action audit: every Google Ads action mapped to a real outcome, primary-for-goal correct, include_in_conversions_metric reviewed.
  • Output: tracking health report with gaps ranked by impact. Gaps block D4 build, not D3 strategy.

D2d Landing Page and Site Audit

  • /ads landing for ads-perspective LP scoring.
  • searchfit-seo:seo-audit for SEO-focused audit. Cross-check with SEMrush siteaudit_research.
  • Core Web Vitals: real user data from Search Console + lab data from PageSpeed Insights.
  • Mobile UX: form friction, tap targets, scroll behavior, sticky CTA, click-to-call.
  • Trust signal audit: certifications above fold, reviews, insurance logos, warranty.
  • Schema audit: LocalBusiness, AutoRepair, FAQPage, Review present or missing.
  • Output: LP backlog scored impact x effort. Feeds D4b build prioritization.

D2e Voice of Customer Research

  • Review scrape: Google, Yelp, Facebook for client AND top 5 competitors via Brightdata. Code reviews by anxiety + persona.

  • Service advisor interview (30 min): top 5 questions, top 5 walk-aways, top 5 referral sources.
  • Call recording analysis: 20 recent inbound calls from CallRail. Code by persona, JTBD, anxiety, outcome.
  • Output: VoC research filed to wiki/voc/ in the Obsidian vault (one note per source: reviews, social listening per platform, service advisor interview, call recording analysis). Cross-referenced to personas in wiki/personas/ and used in content brief. Conversational language extracted as wiki entities for ad copy and LP copy. Run ingest [voc-file] after each source to auto-create cross-references.

D5 - Launch and Stabilize

14. Persistent Knowledge in Obsidian (per-client vault)

Account Strategist inherits the client vault from Sales at D1 (created at S7 alongside the Asana project). The vault is the canonical home for anything that should outlive the engagement, refresh quarterly, or compound across the client lifecycle. Asana holds the operational tasks. Obsidian holds the knowledge.

The persistence boundary: if it should be true a year from now, it lives in Obsidian. If it has a due date, it lives in Asana. If it has a price tag, it lives in invoiced.app. If it has a stage name, it lives in Pipedrive.

14.5 Cross-Project Power Move (point any repo at the vault)

The PSG repo per client (where googleads_psg mutations run) reads from the client vault for context. Add this block to the per-client repo's CLAUDE.md:

# Append to apps/ads/CLAUDE.md or per-client repo CLAUDE.md
## Client Knowledge Vault
Path: ~/Obsidian/psg-[client-slug]-vault

When you need client context not already in this repo:
1. Read wiki/hot.md first (recent context cache)
2. If not enough, read wiki/index.md (master catalog)
3. If you need domain detail, read wiki/personas/ or wiki/voc/
4. If you need decision history, read wiki/decisions/
5. If you need a past experiment, read wiki/experiments/
6. Only then drill into specific wiki pages.

Do NOT read the vault for general coding questions or tasks
unrelated to this client.

14.7 Offboard - Vault Handover

  • If client wants the vault: export full psg-[client-slug]-vault/ as a zip. Include the .obsidian/ folder so they get the dashboard, snippets, and MCP setup. Provide setup instructions.
  • PSG retains a copy of the vault in the PSG-internal vault (separate Obsidian instance) under past-engagements/[client-slug]/ for cross-client learnings.
  • Update PSG-internal playbooks (e.g., collision-repair-content-system, objection library) with any pattern that recurred during this engagement.
  • Remove the MCP entry for the client vault from active Claude Code sessions.

18. Anti-Patterns to Avoid

  • Treating personas as a D3 deliverable that never gets refreshed. VoC has to refresh quarterly in D6d or personas drift.
  • Skipping D2e VoC research because the client says they already know their customer. They know their best customer. They do not know the one who searched and bought elsewhere.
  • Building LPs without persona alignment. Every D4c brief starts from the D3c message-market fit grid.
  • Running --execute without a prior --dry-run. Violates the safety rule in CLAUDE.md.
  • Optimizing within the 14-day stabilization window. Kills smart bidding signal.
  • Killing a CRO experiment before statistical significance. Throws away learning.
  • Running CRO on an LP that has not passed Core Web Vitals. You measure speed, not message.
  • Reporting clicks and impressions only. Report CPA, ROAS, LP CR, and LTV-adjusted return per persona.
  • Building one universal LP for all personas. Insurance claimants and cash-pay customers have opposing anxieties.
  • Trusting SEMrush volumes blindly for very local terms. Cross-check with Google Keyword Planner and Search Console actuals.
  • Forgetting that calls are the primary collision conversion. Click-to-call without offline import means bidding blind.
  • Letting Sanity content go stale because nobody owns the quarterly refresh. D6d has a named Content Lead.
  • Removing PSG card from MCC during offboard before confirming client billing is set up. Ads silently stop.

## Design Context

### Users
Luxury and exotic vehicle owners on Long Island (HHI $200k+, age 38-65). Porsche, BMW, Audi, Aston Martin, Rivian, Lucid owners. Stressed after an accident. Searching on mobile from the road or at home. Secondary: premium daily-driver households (HHI $75-200k). Core job: find a certified shop they can trust without driving to NYC.

### Brand Personality
Heritage-driven. Precision-focused. Quietly confident. 75 years, four generations. Certifications lead — proof before pitch. Voice: formal 3/10, serious 8/10.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light prestige. Off-white (#F7F4EF) base, near-black (#1B1714) text, gold (#c8a94a) accent lines, red (#cc1122) for primary CTAs. Gilda Display serif for headings, Jost sans for body. Generous negative space. Thin gold horizontal rules as structural dividers. Section contrast via warm light gray (#EDE9E3). Real-shop photography, never stock. No flourish, no motion, no gloss.

Anti-references: flashy dealership sites, neon, pastel, stock crash imagery, generic "get your estimate" templates.

### Design Principles
1. **Proof before pitch** — Every claim must be followed immediately by verifiable evidence.
2. **Scarcity creates trust** — "Only shop on Long Island" is the conversion mechanism. Never dilute it.
3. **Restraint is authority** — More whitespace, fewer decorative elements = more prestige.
4. **Dark = precision** — Dark backgrounds signal technical expertise, not just "cool." Consistent with shop/automotive context.
5. **No wasted ink** — Every element on the page earns its place or gets removed.

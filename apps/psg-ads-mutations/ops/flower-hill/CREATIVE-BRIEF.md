# Flower Hill Auto Body — Creative Brief
**Creative approach:** Dual-tier — luxury/exotic prestige + trusted neighborhood shop  
**Key insight:** The exotic certifications are the headline story. They halo the general business. Lead with exotic, convert everyone.

---

## Brand Positioning in Ads

Flower Hill is not a "cheap and fast" shop. Ads must communicate:
- **Precision** — OEM certified, manufacturer-approved
- **Trust** — family-owned, North Shore legacy (75 years, 627 five-star reviews in Roslyn)
- **Range** — from everyday fender benders to Lamborghinis, the quality never changes
- **Exclusivity** — Long Island's only Aston Martin, Rivian, and Lucid certified center

Avoid: generic stock photos, price-leading language, discount framing

### The Trust Gap — Core Messaging Insight
Industry research shows **66% of drivers distrust repair shops** and **2 in 3 U.S. drivers don't trust the industry at all**. This is the single most important purchase barrier. Every ad must answer the trust question before it makes a pitch:
- Certifications = trust proxy (OEM wouldn't certify a shop they don't stand behind)
- 75 years = trust proxy (bad shops don't survive seven decades)
- 627 Roslyn reviews at 4.9★ = trust proxy (customers who came back)
- Family-owned = trust proxy (the Picciano family's name is on the building)

## Customer Personas (from PSG Nassau County Market Analysis)

These five personas map to Nassau's collision repair customer base. Match creative to persona.

| Persona | Who | Ad Approach |
|---------|-----|-------------|
| **Affluent Assured** | Age 35-65, Manhasset/Roslyn/Syosset, luxury vehicle, low deductible, quality-driven | OEM certifications, factory-perfect results, "your car's value protected," white-glove CTA |
| **Budgeting DIYer** | Age 25-60, cost-sensitive, mass-market car, questions every cost | "All insurance accepted," "free estimate," no-surprise pricing messaging |
| **Busy Tech-Savvy Millennial** | Age 25-40, Gen Y/Z, time-constrained, digital-first, researches online | Online estimate form, fast turnaround, "we handle the claim," strong reviews, digital CTA |
| **Cautious Senior** | Age 65+, trust-driven, referral-reliant, not tech-savvy | "Family-owned 75 years," community roots, phone-first CTA, warm/reassuring tone |
| **Mobile Modernist / EV Owner** | Any age, EV owner, convenience-first, tech-forward | EV certification exclusivity, "certified, not just equipped," specialized language |

**Primary ad persona targets:**
- Meta/Instagram: Affluent Assured + Mobile Modernist/EV
- Google Search: All personas (keyword intent self-selects)
- Huntington launch: Busy Tech-Savvy Millennial (digital) + Cautious Senior (trust)

## Content Pillars

| Pillar | What | Platform | Audience |
|--------|------|----------|----------|
| **Exotic Certifications** | Feature the marques by name. Logos where licensed. Close-up shots of exotic vehicles in-shop. | Instagram, Google | Luxury segment |
| **Before / After** | Transformation content. Side-by-side of damage vs. restored. | Meta, Instagram | Both segments |
| **The Repair Process** | Time-lapse or step-by-step of a repair. Builds trust, shows expertise. | Instagram Reels, YouTube | Both segments |
| **Team & Shop** | Real technicians, real facility. No stock photos. | Meta, Google Display | General segment |
| **Reviews / Social Proof** | Highlighted 5-star reviews, especially exotic owners. | Meta, Instagram | Both segments |
| **North Shore Identity** | Tie to community. Three locations. "Your neighbors." | Meta | General segment |
| **Huntington Launch** | "Now open" or "Now serving Huntington" messaging. | Meta, Google | General + luxury |

## Visual Style Guide

| Element | General Tier | Luxury Tier |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| Photography | Clean, bright, real shop | Dark, dramatic, studio-quality car shots |
| Color palette | Brand colors + white | Black, dark grey, gold accents |
| Font weight | Medium | Light/thin (premium feel) |
| Tone | Warm, community-oriented | Precise, exclusive, assured |
| Imagery don'ts | No stock photos, no generic cars | Never show budget vehicles alongside exotics |

## Creative Testing Plan

Month 1: Launch with 2 variants per ad set (A/B static images)  
Month 2: Introduce video; test video vs. static  
Month 3: Test exotic-led headline vs. general-led headline on same audience  
Month 4: UGC/testimonial test vs. polished content  

Kill rule: Any ad with CTR below 1.5% after $100 spend — pause and replace.

# Campaign Brief: Flower Hill Auto Body
**Generated:** May 2026
**Website:** https://www.flowerhillautobody.com

**Objective:** Lead generation (inbound calls + estimate requests)
**Concepts:** 4

## Brand DNA Summary
Flower Hill Auto Body is a 75-year, four-generation Picciano family collision shop on Long Island's North Shore, holding 25+ OEM certifications (including the only Aston Martin, Rivian, and Lucid certified centers on Long Island) and I-CAR Gold Class status. The voice is heritage-driven, precision-focused, and quietly confident (formal 3/10, serious 8/10), pairing charcoal #1a1a1a and accent red #cc1122 with generous negative space and real-shop photography of luxury and exotic vehicles. The audience splits across high-net-worth exotic and EV owners ($200k+ HHI, age 38-65) and premium daily-driver owners ($75-200k HHI, age 28-60) clustered across Roslyn, Glen Cove, and the new Huntington Station location.

## Audit Context
Three signals shape this brief. First, market intelligence confirms zero paid competitors on the highest-intent EV and exotic terms ("rivian certified collision center" 880/mo, "aston martin body shop" $59.86 CPC, "lucid collision repair" $16.72 CPC), creating a short-window arbitrage on owners who currently drive to NYC or NJ. Second, 66% of drivers distrust repair shops, so every concept must lead with proof (OEM certifications, 75-year legacy, Lucid 2026 Shop of the Year, 627 reviews at 4.9★) before pitching. Third, the new Huntington Station location has only 14 reviews against DePalo's 2,593 — Huntington creative cannot lean on review volume and must instead borrow trust from the family legacy and OEM authority that no local competitor can match.

## Design Context

### Users
Two audiences with different access levels:
- **PSG internal team** (psg_admin): Nick and PSG staff reviewing network-wide collision shop performance, market intelligence, customer geography, and marketing data. Daily use. Power users who need density and fast scanning.
- **Shop owners** (shop_owner): Individual collision repair shop operators checking their own metrics -- EMI scores, survey counts, trend data. Weekly use. Need clarity and confidence in what they're seeing.

Both audiences are operators -- practical, time-constrained, decision-oriented. Not casual browsers.

### Brand Personality
**Understated. Precise. Commanding.**

From PSG brand guidelines: "Refinement as discipline, not decoration." The brand is built on "understated luxury, precision craft, and measured confidence." Consistency over noise, practical innovation over trends, thoughtful execution over excess.

Three words: **Authoritative. Clean. Trustworthy.**

Emotional target: Users should feel **confidence and control** -- "I know exactly where I stand and what to do next."

### Aesthetic Direction
**Refine what exists.** The current editorial restraint is correct for the brand. Evolution, not revolution.

- **Theme**: Light. Operators use this during business hours, often on laptops in offices or shops. Paper-white surfaces with navy/graphite text.
- **Typography**: Gotham (headings, navigation, buttons, labels) + Didact Gothic (body, data, reading). Gotham leads, Didact reads. Max weight: Bold (700).
- **Color**: Navy (#1E3A52) as primary authority color. Ember (#B8483E) as single focal accent -- limited to one moment per view (eyebrows, active states, alerts). Teal/Clarity (#0EA5A5) for positive signals. Paper/Bone/Stone neutral progression.
- **Shape**: Sharp corners throughout. No border-radius on cards or containers. Precision geometry reflects brand discipline.
- **Iconography**: Lucide icons, outline-only, inheriting currentColor. Never filled.
- **Density**: Data-forward for admins, scannable for shop owners.

Anti-references: Glassmorphism, dark-mode-with-glows, gradient text, rounded bubbly SaaS dashboards, cards-inside-cards.

### Design Principles
1. **Signal over decoration** -- Every element earns its place. If it doesn't inform a decision, remove it.
2. **Ember is precious** -- Phoenix-red accent used sparingly. One focal moment per view. Overuse kills its power.
3. **Structure creates clarity** -- Consistent spacing, clear type hierarchy, and deliberate whitespace do the design work. No ornament needed.
4. **Confidence through precision** -- Aligned baselines, consistent token usage, and mechanical rhythm convey trustworthiness. Sloppy spacing erodes trust.
5. **Operators, not audiences** -- Design for people who act on data. Every view should answer: "What do I do next?"
